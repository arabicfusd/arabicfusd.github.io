About ICF

ICF was established in 1996 at 4039 Irvington Avenue, Fremont, CA 94538 and has been serving the local community since then. Some of the programs that run under the ICF umbrella are:

  • Social services for the local community

  • Feeding the hungry

  • ICF Tech institute

  • After school activities

  • ILM Academy

  • ICF Weekend School

With increasing demands from parents availing our services above, ICF has formed the ICF Arabic Academy to offer high school accredited Arabic Classes. The goal is to leverage the infrastructure, facilities and experience of running a full time School and ICF Weekend School for the last 16+ years. ICF’s dedicated, enthusiastic and experienced teachers running ILM Academy and ICF Weekend School will help us meet the Arabic Language education needs of our community.


Courses Taught


Arabic 1

This course aims at giving the students an introduction to the speaking, reading and writing of Elementary Arabic language, as one of the most important languages of the world. This course will:

  • Help the students in understanding the basic level Arabic language as a progressive language of Learning and Culture throughout the civilized world
  • Enable them to read, write, understand and speak the most common and basic aspects of the Language.
  • Offer them the opportunity to develop basic working knowledge of the spoken and written Arabic.
  • Make them aware of the essential structure of basic Arabic grammar.

Arabic 2/3

This course aims at giving the students advanced understanding to the speaking, reading and writing conventions of Elementary Arabic language, as one of the most important languages of the world. This course will:

  • Help the students in understanding the basic level Arabic language as a progressive language of Learning and Culture throughout the civilized world
  • Enable them to read, write, understand and speak the most common and basic aspects of the Language.
  • Offer them the opportunity to develop basic working knowledge of the spoken and written Arabic.
  • Make them aware of the essential structure of basic Arabic grammar.
Arabic 3 will be available this coming school year!

Credits

This course will be counted as an high school level foreign language for which the student will receive high school credit once approved by their local school district. Students will not need to take another foreign language in school and can use their free period for any other class or elective of their choice.



FAQ


Is this Arabic program certified by FUSD? What about other school districts?

Yes. If your student is enrolled in Fremont Unified School District, they will receive high school credit from their school district for taking Arabic with ICF Arabic Academy this year. We are in the process of receiving approval from New Haven Unified as well as Newark Unified for the 2016-2017 school year.

How does the course load and requirements compare with other language courses?

The course load and requirements are comparable to other language courses by your local school district. Students will receive 5 credits per semester for taking Arabic--so 10 foreign language credits per year.

What are the goals of this Arabic course?

The course is designed to
-Help students develop the basic Arabic language understanding
-Enable students to read, write, speak, and understand the common aspects of the language
-Offer them an opportunity to develop working knowledge of spoken and written Arabic, and
-Make them aware of the essential structure of the Arabic grammar

What are the options for continuing with Arabic courses, such as Arabic 2 and Arabic 3 to satisfy the high school graduation language requirement?

The UC and CSU school student require 2 years of foreign language in high school, and 3 years are recommended. Every year, we will be adding an Arabic level. We have offered Level 1 and Level 2 in the '16-'17 school year, and have the curriculum finalized to add Level 3 next fall.

Who will be teaching the course?

The lead instructor will be Nahil Ireiqat, a teacher with over 19 years of experience in developing result-driven curriculums and delivering enthusiastic instruction of the foreign language Arabic for elementary, middle, and high school children, she is a dedicated Foreign Language Educator, who was honored as runner-up “Teacher of the Year” for the Santa Clara School District in 2006 and Alameda County in 2013. Nahil is also a member of the CLTA “California Language Teachers' Association.”

How do I officially enroll into the class and have a guaranteed a spot in the class?

Registration for the 2017 -'18 school year is now open! Please Register!

Will the instructors to student ratio be high?

To maintain a high standard of learning and the best classroom experience, we will be keeping a very small student teacher ratio. This is why we have very limited spots and encourage early registration in order to secure a spot. In addition to a main instructor, we will also be having one or more T.A's as needed in the classroom, along with extended office hours outside class times to make sure students are able to get their questions answered in a timely manner. For our first semester, we are capping registration so that the instructors:students ratio is 1:15.

Which textbook will be used for the class?

The textbooks used will be the al-Kitab al-Asasi series, along with supplemental materials and worksheets designed by the instructors.

I am already enrolled into a language class at my school. What do I need to do to enroll into Arabic course instead?

In the beginning of the school year, junior high and high school students in the local school districts are allowed a grace period to drop/add any classes. If your student is interested in taking Arabic, they are allowed to drop their language class (Spanish/French, etc) at their school and enroll in this instead.

What is the cost of the course?

The cost of the program is $450 per semester, $900 a year. This is actually much more affordable than the costs of other similar language programs in the area (Chinese, Hindi, etc).

What are the dates and timings of when the class will be held?

For the '16-'17 school year, classes are currently being held every week. Classes will begin on September 19th. There are four hours of language instruction per week for all courses.

  • For Arabic I, it will be Mon and Wed from 6:30-7:30 PM, and Saturdays 11 AM - 1 PM.

  • For Arabic II, it will be Mon and Wed from 5:00-6:00 PM, and Saturdays 2 PM - 4 PM.
